## Tone of Voice
- **Friendly, not formal** — We talk like a helpful colleague, not a corporate brochure
- **Confident, not arrogant** — We know our product is good, but we let results speak
- **Clear, not clever** — Plain English beats marketing jargon every time
- **Empathetic** — We understand that ads are confusing and expensive
- **Action-oriented** — Every message should move toward a next step

### Words We Use
- "Smart" not "synergistic"
- "Finds wasted spend" not "optimizes budget allocation"
- "More customers" not "increased conversion rates"
- "Plain English" not "natural language processing"
- "Your AI assistant" not "our proprietary technology stack"

### Words We Avoid
- Jargon: "leverage," "synergy," "paradigm," "disrupt"
- Overclaims: "best in class," "world's first," "revolutionary"
- Uncertainty: "might," "could possibly," "we think"
